# Video walkthrough script — TN Assembly 2026

**Target length:** ~6:40 (400 seconds)

**Headline:** In 2026, Tamil Nadu’s assembly became more fragmented: TVK won 108 seats, 163 of 234 constituencies changed hands, and average winning margins fell from 11.8% to 7.7%.

---

## [0:00] Hook — headline (Slide 1, ~30s)

**On screen:** Title slide

**Delivery tip:** Smile, no script reading. Look at camera for first 10 seconds.

Hi — I'm pitching story ideas for AtliQ's Tamil Nadu 2026 election hour. Here's the headline in one sentence: In 2026, Tamil Nadu’s assembly became more fragmented: TVK won 108 seats, 163 of 234 constituencies changed hands, and average winning margins fell from 11.8% to 7.7%. We'll unpack three visuals — margins, seat transitions, and where TVK won — using only Election Commission data.

---

## [0:35] Context — the brief (Slide 2, ~35s)

**On screen:** The brief slide

**Delivery tip:** Keep energy conversational.

AtliQ wants the opposite of a debate show — clean facts, no predictions, no commentary. I'm treating this like a producer briefing: what should go on air, in what order, and what the numbers actually show across all 234 assembly constituencies.

---

## [1:15] Headline numbers (Slide 3, ~45s)

**On screen:** KPI slide — point to each bullet

**Delivery tip:** Count on fingers 1–5. Pause half-second between stats.

Five numbers to anchor the show. First — TVK is recorded as the winner in 108 constituencies. Second — 163 seats, or 69.7 percent, show a different winning party than in 2021. Third — the average winning margin fell from 11.8 percent to 7.7 percent. Fourth — in 61 seats, the winner received less than 35 percent of valid votes — that's a fragmented pattern. And fifth — only 14 seats had a majority winner above 50 percent.

---

## [2:10] Story 1 — margins (Slide 4, ~55s)

**On screen:** Margin chart + optional dashboard Margins page (/margins)

**Delivery tip:** Screen-share dashboard here if recording hybrid.

Story one is about how close races became. This chart compares winning margins in 2021 versus 2026. The statewide average dropped about 4.1 percentage points. For viewers, that means more seats where the runner-up was competitive — good for segment producers who want tension without speculation. We are not explaining why — only showing the distribution shifted down.

---

## [3:20] Story 2 — seat flows (Slide 5, ~60s)

**On screen:** Sankey bar chart or dashboard Seat flows tab

**Delivery tip:** Trace one flow with cursor slowly.

Story two is the transition picture — who held seats in 2021 versus who holds them in 2026. The largest flows in our data include: DMK to TVK in 65 seats; DMK to DMK in 40 seats; AIADMK to TVK in 26 seats; AIADMK to AIADMK in 22 seats. In the interactive dashboard, the Sankey view shows this as flows between party labels — normalized for long ECI party names. Language matters: say constituencies changed hands, not voters defected.

---

## [4:35] Story 3 — vote share & TVK (Slide 6, ~50s)

**On screen:** Vote share chart

**Delivery tip:** Neutral tone — no praise or criticism of any party.

Story three is TVK's footprint and statewide vote share shifts. This bar chart compares top parties' share of valid votes in 2021 and 2026. TVK's regional seat concentration includes Chennai Metro (29 seats), South (26 seats), Kongu (16 seats). We report where TVK won and how shares changed — we do not claim whose votes moved, because that would require voter-level data we don't have.

---

## [5:40] Geographic mosaic (Slide 7, ~35s)

**On screen:** Mosaic slide or dashboard Mosaic tab

**Delivery tip:** Quick pass — don't linger on every region.

For geography, we use a regional mosaic — each square is one constituency, same visual weight. That avoids the misleading big-map problem where land area dominates. Producers can zoom Chennai Metro, Kongu, or South blocks for B-roll. The pattern is descriptive: where colors cluster, not why.

---

## [6:25] Editorial recommendation (Slide 8, ~45s)

**On screen:** Editorial recommendation slide

**Delivery tip:** This is the scored slide — be crisp and confident.

My recommendation for the 60-minute show: open twelve minutes on statewide arithmetic — TVK seats, flip count, margin shift. Then eighteen minutes on regional mosaic plus transition flows. Twelve minutes on the closest races — use the explorer table for margins under five percent. Close eight minutes on data caveats — live ECI versus final Form-20, and how we normalized party labels. That order moves from simple to detailed, and ends with trust.

---
