Story one is about how close races became. This chart compares winning margins in 2021 versus 2026. The statewide average dropped about 4.1 percentage points. For viewers, that means more seats where the runner-up was competitive — good for segment producers who want tension without speculation. Slide 5 · 3:20 · ~60s · Story 2 — seat flows

Story two is the transition picture — who held seats in 2021 versus who holds them in 2026. The largest flows in our data include: DMK to TVK in 65 seats; DMK to DMK in 40 seats; AIADMK to TVK in 26 seats; AIADMK to AIADMK in 22 seats. In the interactive dashboard, the Sankey view shows this as flows between party labels — normalized for long ECI party names. Language matters: say constituencies changed hands, not voters defected.

Tip: Trace one flow with cursor slowly.

Slide 6 · 4:35 · ~50s · Story 3 — vote share & TVK

Story three is TVK's footprint and statewide vote share shifts. This bar chart compares top parties' share of valid votes in 2021 and 2026. TVK's regional seat concentration includes Chennai Metro (29 seats), South (26 seats), Kongu (16 seats). We report where TVK won and how shares changed — we do not claim whose votes moved, because that would require voter-level data we don't have.
